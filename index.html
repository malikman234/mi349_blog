10 RA Approved Dorm Essentials for First Years Students

By Mohamed Malik Published on October 14th

1. Fan

Unless you live in a fairly new dorm, you might be out of luck when it comes to air conditioning. The first month after move in season tends to be the hottest time in the dorms. The rooms tend to not have the best ventilation, so think about investing in a box fan. They are great for circulating air and easy to set up. I recommend cracking the window and putting the fan in front of it, especially at night.

2. Minifridge

The dining halls can have funky hours and there will be times when you miss the dining window. Mini Fridges are great because you can eat or have a snack whenever you please! Pro tip, get one with a separate freezer because then you can microwave mac and cheese at 2am.

3. Shower shoes

If you have community restrooms it's not the end of the world! I actually prefer them because they get cleaned daily. I do not trust 3 other college aged boys to keep a bathroom clean. Shower shoes are essential because the showers are used by 50+ people a day. The last thing you want are warts or a fungal infection!

4. Extension cords

Depending on how you arrange the room, the power outlets might be inaccessible. Extension cords easily solve this problem so you can charge your devices no matter where you are.

5. Multiple sets of sheets and towels

When it comes to laundry in college, letting it pile up can be super easy! Don't fall into this trap, do your laundry regularly. Also, you should have multiple sets of towels and sheets so that you have clean ones to use while the others are in the wash.

6. Snacks

The minifridge is one thing, but stocking it regularly is another thing. Having all the snacks you like are great for when you're studying, about to leave for class or when you're just hanging out. Also your guests will greatly appreciate them!

7. Futon

Speaking of guests, futons are great for additional seating or a place for a friend to crash. The dual functionality is great for small spaces like dorm rooms.

8. Shower robe

This is great if you have suite style or community restrooms. A robe is a super comfy and easy way to cover yourself up between the bathroom and your room.

9. Extra Storage

Dorms are notorious for being super small, especially when you have a roommate. Invest in some type of storage solution like these cubes or under bed storage, you will thank me later.

10. Brita

Having water easily accessible to you is something that we tend to take for granted. Sometimes you don't want to go all the way down the hall to the drinking fountain. That's why britas or any other water filtering system are great for residents.

What I wish I knew about choosing a major sooner!

By Mohamed Malik Published on October 14th

The idea of going off to college is something that high school seniors all around the country rejoice over. The allure of complete freedom and a fresh beginning somewhere new is completely understandable. However, something that can often be forgotten about in all the fun is the academics. Ultimately the main reason you pursue higher education is to learn, develop new skills and ultimately secure a job afterwards.

Coming into university, I had a complete plan for what I would study and how my future would look like. That was up until I finished my first year and decided that business wasn't for me. I was left feeling so lost and had completed credits for a degree that I no longer wanted.

Pro tip #1: Don’t take a semester worth of major specific courses unless you are 100% set on that path. Take a few exploratory classes and the rest should be prerequisites for your first year.

Eventually I found advertising and I thought that was the new path forward. I started fresh taking all the classes required and after the fall semester, I wasn’t convinced but I continued with the classes for spring semester anyways.

Pro tip # 2: Trust your gut! If you don’t enjoy what you are doing, make that change. It’s better to be at the bottom of a ladder you want to be on, rather than at the top of one you don’t want to be on.

I spent that summer doing so much research and soul searching into what I actually wanted to pursue. By a stroke of pure luck, I found UI/UX design and I instantly knew this was for me. It was the intersection of all the things I loved.

Pro tip # 3: Set up informal interviews with alumni either virtually or in person to ask all the questions you want to know about the real working world. They were in your shoes once and a lot of people want to give back.

Choosing your major can be very stressful, but you just need to be patient with yourself. It is completely normal to change your major once or even several times. Don’t be afraid to explore, that is what college is for!
